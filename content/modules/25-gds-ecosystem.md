## Learning Objectives
- Understand how GDS fits alongside NDC, APIs, and direct airline channels
- Know what OTAs are and how they differ from traditional agencies
- Recognize industry disruptions and trends affecting GDS professionals

## Overview
The GDS isn't the only way to book flights anymore. Airlines now sell directly through their websites, OTAs (MakeMyTrip, Expedia) use APIs, and NDC (New Distribution Capability) is changing how airline content is distributed. Understanding this landscape helps you see where GDS expertise fits in the evolving industry — and why it remains valuable even as technology shifts.

## Key Concepts

### Concept 1: OTAs (Online Travel Agencies)
MakeMyTrip, Goibibo, Expedia, Booking.com — these are OTAs. They sell flights (and hotels, cars) directly to consumers through websites and apps. Many use GDS behind the scenes but also connect directly to airlines. OTAs have commoditized simple bookings — which is why GDS agents add value on complex itineraries.

### Concept 2: NDC (New Distribution Capability)
NDC is an IATA standard that lets airlines distribute rich content (bundled fares, ancillaries, personalized offers) directly to agencies without traditional GDS intermediation. It's a modernization — instead of text-based fare displays, NDC enables visual, API-based content. Airlines like Lufthansa and British Airways are pushing NDC.

### Concept 3: Airline Direct Channels
Airlines sell through their own websites (airindia.com, emirates.com) and bypass the GDS entirely. They keep more revenue this way (no GDS booking fees). However, agencies and GDS still handle 40-60% of bookings globally, especially for corporate and complex travel.

### Concept 4: APIs in Travel
APIs (Application Programming Interfaces) allow software systems to talk to each other. Amadeus offers APIs for search, booking, and ticketing that OTAs and corporate tools use. At Coforge, you may work with clients that use Amadeus APIs integrated into their own travel management software.

### Concept 5: Industry Disruption
Low-cost carriers disrupted full-service airlines. OTAs disrupted traditional agencies. NDC is disrupting GDS distribution. Mobile apps are disrupting desktop booking. The industry constantly evolves — GDS professionals who adapt (learning APIs, NDC, multi-source content) remain valuable.

### Concept 6: Why GDS Still Matters
GDS excels at: (1) comparing multiple airlines in one search, (2) complex multi-city international itineraries, (3) corporate travel management with policy enforcement, (4) full-service booking including SSRs, EMDs, and post-ticketing changes. For simple point-to-point domestic flights, OTAs may suffice. For everything else, GDS expertise is essential.

## Step-by-Step Guide
1. **Understand the landscape:** GDS is one channel among many — direct, OTA, NDC, API.
2. **Know your value:** GDS agents add value on complex bookings, corporate travel, and post-sales service.
3. **Learn NDC basics:** It's not replacing GDS, but complementing it. Some fares are only available via NDC.
4. **Understand OTA limitations:** OTAs handle simple bookings well but struggle with complex changes, refunds, and SSRs.
5. **Stay current:** Follow IATA and Amadeus announcements about NDC and API developments.
6. **Position yourself:** GDS + NDC knowledge makes you more valuable than GDS-only or NDC-only.

## Real-World Scenario
**Situation:** In an interview, you're asked: "With OTAs and airline direct websites, why do we still need GDS professionals?"

**Question:** How do you answer?

**Answer:** "Simple bookings — one person, one return flight — can be done on MakeMyTrip or the airline's website. But corporate travel with 50 employees, each with different policies, frequent flyer programs, meal preferences, and complex multi-city itineraries with connections — that requires a GDS. Add post-ticketing services like exchanges, refunds, schedule change management, and EMDs — OTAs often struggle with these. GDS professionals manage the full lifecycle, not just the booking. That's why Amadeus still processes billions of transactions and why companies like Coforge need GDS-trained professionals."

## Common Mistakes
- **Mistake 1:** Thinking GDS will disappear because of OTAs
  - *Why it matters:* It leads to career anxiety and undervaluing your skills
  - *Correct approach:* GDS handles ~40% of global bookings and is essential for corporate/complex travel

- **Mistake 2:** Ignoring NDC developments
  - *Why it matters:* Some airline fares (especially Lufthansa Group) are only available through NDC channels
  - *Correct approach:* Learn how NDC works alongside GDS — agencies increasingly need both

- **Mistake 3:** Not understanding how OTAs work
  - *Why it matters:* Customers compare your service to OTA prices and convenience
  - *Correct approach:* Know OTA strengths (convenience, price comparison) and weaknesses (limited post-sales support)

## Key Glossary Terms
- **OTA (Online Travel Agency):** A website/app that sells travel directly to consumers (MakeMyTrip, Expedia)
- **NDC (New Distribution Capability):** An IATA standard for modern, API-based airline content distribution
- **API (Application Programming Interface):** A software interface that lets different systems communicate and share data
- **Direct Channel:** An airline's own website or app where they sell without intermediaries
- **Aggregator:** A platform that compiles travel options from multiple sources (GDS, NDC, direct) into one display

## Interview Tip
Say: "I understand that GDS is part of a larger ecosystem including NDC, APIs, and direct channels. GDS professionals add value in complex bookings, corporate travel, and post-sales service — areas where OTAs and direct channels fall short."
